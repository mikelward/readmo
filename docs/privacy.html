Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 27, 2026

Readmo is a mobile-first reader for the RSS, Atom, and JSON feeds you subscribe to. This policy explains what information Readmo collects, why, and how it is handled. Readmo is operated by Mikel Ward (mikelward.com).

Information we collect

  • Account information. When you sign in, Readmo uses a third-party identity provider (for example, Google) through Supabase Auth. We receive a basic profile from that provider — typically your email address and a user identifier. We do not receive your password.
  • Your reading data. The feeds you subscribe to, the folders you create, and per-item state (read, opened, pinned, favorited, done, hidden) are stored so they can sync across your devices.
  • Feed content. Readmo fetches and stores the articles published by the feeds you add — including private or tokenized feeds (for example, personal newsletter or secret feed URLs) when you choose to add them — so they can be shown to you and read offline. Secret feed URLs are kept server-side and are not exposed to other users.
  • Technical data. Standard request data (such as IP address and timestamps) is processed by our hosting providers to deliver and secure the service.

How we use information

  • To authenticate you and keep your account secure.
  • To provide the reader and sync your subscriptions and reading state across your devices.
  • To fetch, store, and display the feeds you subscribe to.
  • To notify the operator when a new account is created, so the service can be maintained.

Readmo does not sell your personal information, and does not use your data for advertising.

Where your data is stored

Readmo relies on third-party infrastructure to operate:

  • Supabase — database, authentication, and server-side feed polling.
  • Vercel — hosting and delivery of the web app.
  • Your identity provider (e.g. Google) — for sign-in. Their handling of your data is governed by their own privacy policy.
  • Jina Reader (optional) — when a site blocks Readmo's normal request, a feed or page URL you add may be retrieved through the Jina Reader service as a fallback. This is used only when configured, and only for that fetch fallback.

Your reading data is isolated per user. Other signed-in users cannot read your subscriptions, folders, or item state.

Feed publishers

When Readmo fetches a feed, the request goes to that publisher's servers. Content shown in Readmo belongs to its original publishers, and Readmo always links back to the source. Publishers may log Readmo's requests as they would any other reader.

Cookies and local storage

Readmo uses cookies and on-device storage only to keep you signed in and to cache content for offline reading and performance. It does not use third-party advertising or tracking cookies. On a shared device, cached content is cleared when you sign out or switch accounts.

Data retention and deletion

Your account and reading data are kept while your account is active. You can request deletion of your account and associated data at any time by contacting us at the address below; we will remove your personal data within a reasonable period.

Children

Readmo is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the date at the top of this page.
